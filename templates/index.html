The Story of Sava Hurulbaev

A Social Entrepreneur Bridging Global Experience with Local Change

I See Myself as a Social Entrepreneur

My mission has always been to fill the gaps my community faces day to day — gaps in education, in leadership, in opportunity. Where some see problems, I see possibilities. Entrepreneurs are best positioned to solve community challenges because they are trained to recognize problems not as obstacles, but as opportunities for innovation. Once equipped with the right knowledge and global perspective, they can bring the world back into their communities — inspiring, motivating, and driving real change.

Laying the Foundations: Big Ben Education Hub

In 2020, I established Big Ben Education Hub with a bold vision: to give young people access to quality education and the confidence to participate in shaping their futures. Despite the challenges of the time, within the very first year we welcomed over 400 students. This success demonstrated not only the demand for such a space, but also the power of education as a springboard for transformation.

Scaling Impact: EU Project in Fergana Valley

Soon after, I was invited to serve as Project Manager for one of the largest EU-funded initiatives in Uzbekistan’s Fergana Valley. With a budget of €329,000, my role was to design and deliver 12 youth-led mini-projects across Andijan, Asaka, and seven neighborhoods. For two years, I worked closely with young leaders, training them to engage in local decision-making and ensuring their voices were heard. Watching these young people grow into active changemakers convinced me that empowered communities are built by empowered individuals.

Learning and Bringing Back: From Andijan to Austin

In 2022, I was selected for the Uzbekistan Business Leaders (UBL) Program, funded by the American Councils. Out of 300 applicants, I was chosen as one of the finalists. This fellowship took me to Austin, Texas, where I immersed myself in entrepreneurial sessions at the University of Texas. I saw firsthand how American entrepreneurs innovate, collaborate, and solve problems at scale. Upon returning, I launched the Andijan Business Club, gathering over 60 entrepreneurs. Together with local universities, we created a platform where business leaders shared real-life cases with students, while identifying and nurturing new talent. This became a powerful bridge between education and entrepreneurship.

Chevening Scholar: From London to Local Action

In 2023, I was awarded the Chevening Scholarship — one of the most prestigious UK Government awards. Only 1–2% of all applicants worldwide succeed, and I was honored to be among them. Through Chevening, I completed my Master’s in Entrepreneurship, Innovation, and Enterprise Development in London (2023–2024). But my ambition was never just to study — I wanted to act. Returning to Uzbekistan, I opened the second branch of Big Ben Education Hub, which became more than a learning center. It is now a hybrid ecosystem: - A coworking space for entrepreneurs A training hub for English teachers to grow skills and income - A networking zone for students and business leaders to connect, collaborate, and create opportunities together This reflects my philosophy: education and entrepreneurship together are the engines of social chan

Andijan Business Leaders Program: Multiplying Change

Inspired by my U.S. experience, I launched the Andijan Business Leaders Program. Unlike programs that target students alone, this initiative empowers young entrepreneurs who each employ 40+ people across industries such as pharmaceuticals, textiles, IT, hospitality, education, furniture, and real estate. - In 2024 and 2025, I led 20 entrepreneurs on 10-day leadership journeys in London. These were not just trips — they were accelerators. Participants returned with global best practices, stronger networks, and fresh strategies to apply in their businesses and communities. - In 2026, I will take 10 outstanding entrepreneurs to the United States, connecting Andijan’s leaders with global markets and networks. This multiplies their ability to improve industries, generate jobs, and solve pressing community challenges.

My Philosophy: Change Takes Steps

Real change does not happen overnight. It requires influence, impact, and persistence. 1. Influence minds with education and exposure. 2. Affect systems through leadership and collaboration. 3. Create impact that transforms communities and industries. Every project I lead — in education, entrepreneurship, or leadership development — follows this process. By educating and empowering entrepreneurs, I am building the capacity of those who can solve the most pressing challenges our communities face. I believe the future of Andijan, and Uzbekistan as a whole, lies in its entrepreneurs — educated, globally aware, and locally committed. They are the ones who will see every problem as an opportunity and transform challenges into stepping stones toward progress.
